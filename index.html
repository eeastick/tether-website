What Tether does

For owners

Heart disease means frequent check-ins, anxious nights, and the constant question of whether your dog is okay right now. Tether monitors continuously — so you're not flying blind between appointments.

For vets

Clinical decisions deserve clinical data. Tether gives cardiologists and internists continuous insight into cardiac and respiratory function — without hospitalizing the patient to get it.

How it works

01

Implanted once

A small device placed subcutaneously in the left thorax — a routine procedure for any vet familiar with implantables.

02

Senses continuously

Tether tracks cardiac rhythm, respiratory impedance, and body temperature during rest — when the signal is clearest and most clinically meaningful.

03

Delivers insight

Data syncs to the owner's phone and the vet dashboard automatically. Trends surface early. Alerts reach the right people before a crisis develops.
